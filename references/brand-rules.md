# Brand rules — the enforceable checklist the copy gate runs

The condensed, enforceable subset of the workspace brand/editorial rules the skill applies to every
generated string. Run this as a checklist over each draft. Full source of truth is the workspace
`CLAUDE.md`; this is the operational shortlist.

## Company & product naming

- **Tenable** — initial cap in all running text. Never the lowercase `tenable` wordmark in prose.
- **Product names** — spell out in **full on first reference**, then short form (e.g. "Tenable One").
  Initial caps in prose, slides, and docs.
- **Never abbreviate** externally: no `VM`, `RBVM`, `TVM`, `Tenable.io`, `.asm`, `.cs`, `WAS`.
  When you hit one, expand to the full **product name** (initial caps, a product — *not* the
  lowercase "vulnerability management" concept): `VM`/`TVM`/`Tenable.io` → **Tenable Vulnerability
  Management**; `WAS` → **Tenable Web App Scanning**; `.cs` → **Tenable Cloud Security**; `.asm` →
  **Tenable Attack Surface Management**; `RBVM` → risk-based vulnerability management (a category,
  lowercase). If you can't map it confidently, flag `brand-rule-trip` rather than guess.
- **Don't modify the Tenable name**: no hyphen, slash, acronym, or compound.
- Don't show the brand name twice on one canvas; don't over-brand (logo **or** lockup, not both).
- **Hexa AI** — keep the space and both caps ("Hexa AI," never "HexaAI" or "hexa ai").
- Don't personify hardware/devices/companies.

## Casing (exact)

- **Lowercase:** exposure management, exposure management platform/program, cloud, internet, open
  source, appsec, infosec, security posture, shadow AI, shadow IT, defense in depth, container,
  data set, vulnerability management.
- **Named exposure solutions — initial caps:** Cloud Exposure, Identity Exposure.
- **Always initial caps:** Active Directory (AD), Kubernetes (K8s), **Predictive Prioritization**
  (always the "z" spelling), NVD, CVSS, CVE, MITRE ATT&CK framework.
- **DevOps** (D, O); **DevSecOps** (D, S, O).
- **Spell out first, acronym after:** AI, IoT, OT, ICS, IaC, SIEM, SOC, MSSPs, CTEM, NGFWs, NOC, BYOD.

## One-word compounds & noun/adjective forms

- One word: cyberattack, cybersecurity, cybercrime, cybercriminals, cyberspace, cyberthreat.
  Two words: cyber risk, cyber insurance.
- **zero day** (noun) / **zero-day** (adj); **zero trust** (noun) / **zero-trust** (adj).
- **on-prem / on-premises** OK; **never "on-premise."**

## Mechanics

- Oxford comma; active voice; personal pronouns OK.
- **Common contractions** wherever they read naturally (it's, they're, you'll, don't).
- **Smart/curly quotes** always — never straight quotes.
- **Em dash spaced on both sides ( — ) and minimized.** Hard cap **one** em dash per short-form post
  (LinkedIn, X, Slack). Longer copy: minimize, don't count.
- **"and,"** not "&" (except inside a company name).
- **`%`** not "percent"; **`#1`** not "number one"; one space after periods.
- Numbers: spell out **zero through nine**; numerals for **10 and up.**
- Headlines/subheads: **sentence case.** One CTA per piece.
- **Hyperlink** any statistic, quotation, or claim.
- No insensitive terms: **allowlist/blocklist**, not whitelist/blacklist.

## Avoid these phrases

- "eliminate/eradicate exposures," "eliminate risk" (we reduce/manage, not eliminate)
- "legacy vulnerabilities," "exposure risk," "cyber exposure" (as a category name)
- "rapid" as filler; "percent" (use `%`)
